lex Dataset Analysis

12/11/2025
This report presents 3 key findings from an analysis of lex. The analysis addressed 6 questions focused on exploring patterns, relationships, and insights within the data.

This line chart tracks life expectancy from 1900 to 2025 for the five countries that experienced the greatest improvements, led by South Korea with a remarkable 59-year increase. All five nations—South Korea, Bangladesh, Iran, North Korea, and Kuwait—started with life expectancies below 30 years in 1900 and have converged to between 74 and 84 years by 2025, demonstrating dramatic health and development gains over the past century.

This line chart displays life expectancy trends for the five countries with the smallest improvements between 1900 and 2025, all located in Sub-Saharan Africa. These nations, including Lesotho, Central African Republic, and Mozambique, gained only 16 to 26 years of life expectancy over the period—less than half the gains seen in top-improving countries—with Lesotho notably showing stagnation and decline in recent decades likely due to the HIV/AIDS epidemic.
