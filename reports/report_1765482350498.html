This report presents 2 key findings from an analysis of lex. The analysis addressed 4 questions focused on exploring patterns, relationships, and insights within the data.

This bar chart ranks the top 10 countries with the largest life expectancy improvements from 1800 to 2100. Kuwait leads with a remarkable 67.4-year gain, followed closely by South Korea (66.6 years) and Singapore (65.7 years), with several Asian, European, and Middle Eastern nations showing gains exceeding 62 years over this three-century span.

This line chart traces the life expectancy trajectories of the top 10 most improved countries from 1800 to 2100. All countries started with similar low life expectancies around 25-30 years in 1800, but show dramatic upward curves particularly after 1900, converging toward 90+ years by the projected end date, with Singapore reaching the highest projected life expectancy of nearly 95 years.
